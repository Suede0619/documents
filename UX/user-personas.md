# User Personas: The Rumi App
## Research-Backed UX Personas for Product Development

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** January 12, 2026
**Research Period:** July 2024 - January 2026
**Methodology:** Triangulated data from poetry reader demographics (NEA), meditation app user studies, spiritual seeker typologies (Bhagavad Gita framework), and competitor user research

---

## RESEARCH FOUNDATION

### Data Sources
- **Poetry Readership Studies**: National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) 2017-2023 surveys
- **Meditation App Analytics**: Calm (4M subscribers), Headspace (2.8M), Insight Timer (26M users)
- **Spiritual Seeker Typology**: Bhagavad Gita framework (4 types of seekers)
- **Demographic Trends**: 60% female poetry readers, 25-34 age group growth, cross-cultural appeal
- **Psychographic Patterns**: Seeking connection, healing, depth, authenticity, meaning during upheaval

### Key Findings Informing Personas
1. **Poetry readers** (22.4M U.S. adults) seek solace during societal/personal upheaval, prefer intimacy over generic content
2. **Meditation app users** experiencing fatigue with generic apps (Calm/Headspace declining downloads/subscribers)
3. **Spiritual seekers** fall into 4 types: Material (pleasure-driven), Knowledge (understanding-driven), Distressed (crisis-driven), Enlightened (surrender-driven)
4. **Gender skew**: 60%+ women in poetry + meditation app audiences
5. **Age sweet spot**: 25-44 for both poetry reading and mindfulness app engagement
6. **Education correlation**: Higher education levels correlate with poetry reading and spiritual seeking

## PERSONA 1: THE DEPTH SEEKER

### Demographics
**Name:** Maya Chen
**Age:** 32
**Location:** Portland, OR
**Occupation:** Product Designer at tech company
**Education:** BA in Visual Arts, MFA in Design
**Income:** $95,000/year
**Relationship Status:** Partnered, no children
**Tech Savviness:** High (early adopter, uses 15+ apps daily)

### Photo/Mood
*[Placeholder: Professional woman, 30s, contemplative expression, warm natural lighting, sitting with tea and journal]*

### Background Story
Maya has been meditating for 5 years, starting with Headspace during a stressful product launch. She graduated to Calm, then tried Insight Timer, but found herself overwhelmed by choice and frustrated by the lack of depth. She owns three Coleman Barks Rumi books and often screenshots quotes to her notes app, but feels disconnected from the fragments. She's searching for a spiritual practice that combines the convenience of an app with the authenticity of traditional texts.

### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal**: Develop a sustainable daily spiritual practice grounded in authentic tradition, not generic mindfulness
- **Secondary Goal**: Understand mystical poetry as a living teaching, not just pretty quotes
- **Underlying Motivation**: Move beyond "stress relief" to genuine spiritual transformation
- **Aspiration**: Connect to something timeless and transcendent in her modern, tech-saturated life

### Pain Points & Frustrations
1. **Generic meditation apps** feel shallow and repetitive — "I've heard '10-minute stress relief' 1,000 times"
2. **Quote apps** decontextualize Rumi into Instagram fodder — "I want the real teaching, not memes"
3. **Insight Timer's 190K tracks** paralyze her with choice — "Where do I even start?"
4. **Lack of structure**: No clear path from beginner to deeper practice
5. **Cultural appropriation concerns**: Worried about "consuming" Rumi without respecting Sufi tradition
6. **Time scarcity**: Needs brevity (10-15 min/day max) but refuses to sacrifice depth

### Behaviors & Preferences
- **App Usage**: Opens meditation app 5-6 days/week, usually morning before work or evening wind-down
- **Reading Habits**: Reads poetry collections in bed, highlights passages, journals reflections on weekends
- **Learning Style**: Visual + reflective; loves beautiful design but needs substance beneath aesthetics
- **Tech Expectations**: Seamless UX, dark mode, offline access, no notifications unless gentle/meaningful
- **Social Sharing**: Rarely shares publicly but saves favorites for personal reflection
- **Payment Willingness**: Will pay $69.99/year for quality content (currently pays for Calm, considering canceling)

### Quote That Captures Mindset
> "I don't want meditation to make me a better employee. I want it to make me a more awake human being. Rumi's poetry touches something real in me, but I don't know how to turn those moments into a practice."

### How Maya Would Use The Rumi App

**Discovery & Onboarding:**
- Downloads after seeing friend's recommendation or App Store search for "authentic Rumi"
- Immediately drawn to design quality (evaluates apps visually first)
- Completes onboarding, intrigued by "sanctuary not feed" messaging

**Daily Habit (Primary Use Case):**
- Opens app each morning with coffee
- Reads daily verse in all three formats (Farsi for beauty, English for understanding, modern retelling for accessibility)
- Spends 5-7 minutes in contemplation mode, sometimes journals her reflections
- Saves favorite verses to personal collection

**Guided Journeys (Monthly):**
- Tries 1-2 guided journeys per month when she has 10-15 minutes
- Particularly drawn to themes of "surrender" and "letting go" during work stress
- Appreciates 5-minute structure (not too long)

**Masnavi Library (Occasional Deep Dives):**
- Browses library on Sunday mornings when she has 30-60 minutes
- Searches by keyword ("longing," "grief," "joy") when experiencing specific emotions
- Reads verses in context (surrounding verses), appreciates scholarly depth

**Conversion Trigger:**
- Hits paywall when trying first guided journey (Day 3-5)
- Converts to premium annual ($69.99) because:
  1. Daily verse alone provides value (builds trust)
  2. Wants to support authentic content creation
  3. Recognizes this is "the real Rumi, not quotes"
  4. Comparable to Calm pricing but vastly superior depth

**Long-Term Retention:**
- High retention (80%+ chance of renewing) if:
  - Daily verse remains consistently meaningful
  - New guided journeys added quarterly
  - Design/UX stays beautiful and glitch-free
- Risk of churn if:
  - Content feels repetitive after 6-9 months
  - App becomes "just another meditation app"

### Design Implications for Maya
- **Elegant, minimal UI**: No clutter, generous white space, contemplative colors
- **Quality over quantity**: Curated daily verse beats overwhelming library
- **Depth indicators**: Show her the 25,000 verses exist without demanding she read them all
- **Beautiful typography**: Serif fonts for Rumi, careful Farsi rendering
- **Offline mode**: Must-have for her (travels for work)
- **No gamification**: Streaks/badges would feel manipulative and turn her off

Robert has taught Rumi in his World Literature class for 15 years. He owns every Coleman Barks translation, Arberry's scholarly edition, and several books on Sufi history. He reads poetry every morning but feels guilty that his Rumi books sit on the shelf most days — he loves them but doesn't make time. ### Goals & Motivations
- **Primary Goal**: Turn his love of Rumi into a consistent daily practice, not just occasional reading
- **Secondary Goal**: Deepen his understanding of the Masnavi beyond the excerpts in Barks' books
- **Teaching Goal**: Discover new verses and stories to share with his students
- **Underlying Motivation**: Model contemplative living for his kids in a distracted world

### Pain Points & Frustrations
1. **Time scarcity**: Mornings are rushed (family breakfast, work prep), evenings exhausted
2. **Book overwhelm**: The Masnavi is 25,000 verses — "Where do I start? How do I navigate?"
3. **Lack of guidance**: No teacher or structure to help him progress through the text
4. **Fragmentation**: Quote apps feel disrespectful to Rumi's artistry and Sufi tradition
5. **Tech skepticism**: Worried apps will "cheapen" the poetry or add distracting features
6. **Limited budget**: Teacher salary means he's price-sensitive ($5-7/month max)

- **Morning Routine**: Coffee, news, wishes he had time for poetry/reflection
- **Tech Usage**: Smartphone for email/texts, rarely downloads new apps, no social media
- **Learning Style**: Text-based, prefers reading over audio, enjoys context and scholarly notes
- **Teaching**: Shares favorite verses with students, loves discussing interpretations
- **Payment Willingness**: Will pay $59.99/year if convinced of value (price-sensitive due to income)

### Quote That Captures Mindset
> "I've loved Rumi for two decades, but I've never read the whole Masnavi. I want to be the kind of person who has a daily practice with this poetry, not just someone who owns the books."

### How Robert Would Use The Rumi App

**Discovery & Onboarding:**
- Discovers through education blog or teacher forum (word-of-mouth in his community)
- Downloads skeptically, prepared to delete if it feels gimmicky
- Pleasantly surprised by scholarly tone and complete Masnavi library

**Daily Habit (Primary Use Case):**
- Sets notification for 6:15 AM (before family wakes)
- Reads daily verse with coffee in 3-5 minutes
- Particularly loves modern retellings (makes ancient wisdom accessible)
- Occasionally reads Farsi aloud to appreciate sound (doesn't understand it but loves the beauty)

**Masnavi Library (Weekly Deep Dives):**
- Spends Sunday afternoons exploring library (30-45 minutes)
- Browses by book/chapter to read Masnavi chronologically
- Searches by theme when preparing lesson plans ("love," "teacher/student," "transformation")
- Saves verses to collections organized by teaching topics

**Guided Journeys (Rarely):**
- Not his primary use case (prefers reading to guided meditation)
- Might try 1-2 journeys out of curiosity but won't seek them regularly

**Conversion Trigger:**
- Hits paywall when trying to access full Masnavi (Day 5-7)
- Hesitates at $69.99/year (more than he'd prefer)
- Converts because:
  1. No other app offers complete Masnavi with translations
  2. Cheaper than buying more Rumi books
  3. Daily verse alone worth $5/month
  4. Wants to support scholars/translators creating this content
- May wait for sale or annual vs. monthly to reduce cost

**Long-Term Retention:**
- High retention (75%+ renewal) if:
  - Content remains scholarly and respectful
  - New modern retellings added (keeps it fresh)
  - App maintains simplicity (no bloat)
- Risk of churn if:
  - Price increases beyond $80/year
  - App adds social/gamification features he finds distracting
  - Better free alternatives emerge

### Design Implications for Robert
- **Text-first interface**: Reading is primary, audio/video secondary
- **Scholarly credibility**: Show translation sources, author credits, context
- **Simple navigation**: Book/chapter structure mirrors physical Masnavi
- **Search by theme**: Organized taxonomy (love, separation, teacher, journey, etc.)
- **Save/organize**: Collections for personal study and teaching prep
- **Minimal distractions**: No pop-ups, ads, or social features
- **Pricing transparency**: Clearly show value (# of verses, new content schedule)

- **Primary Goal**: Transition from clinical mindfulness (stress management) to authentic spiritual practice
- **Secondary Goal**: Explore Sufi mysticism and Persian poetry as paths to meaning

2. **Western mindfulness appropriation**: Frustrated by decontextualized meditation stripped of spiritual roots
3. **Instagram Rumi quotes**: Beautiful but shallow — "I want the real tradition, not influencer spirituality"
4. **Lack of BIPOC representation**: Meditation apps feel "unbearably white" (her words)
5. **Affordability**: $69-129/year subscriptions strain nonprofit salary

- **Social Sharing**: Shares meaningful quotes to Instagram stories, engages with spiritual communities online
- **Payment Willingness**: $60-70/year max (price-sensitive but will pay for authentic, diverse content)

### Quote That Captures Mindset
> "I'm tired of meditation apps telling me to 'optimize my stress response.' I want to explore the mystery of being alive. Rumi's poetry makes me feel connected to something ancient and true."

### How Aisha Would Use The Rumi App

**Discovery & Onboarding:**
- Discovers via Instagram ad or influencer partnership (visual platform fits her discovery style)
- Attracted to "sanctuary not feed" + emphasis on authentic tradition
- Converts quickly if onboarding includes Rumi's story and Sufi context (education matters to her)

**Daily Habit (Primary Use Case):**
- Opens app during morning commute (subway) or evening wind-down
- Reads daily verse, especially drawn to modern first-person retellings (narrative accessibility)
- Sometimes listens to audio if available (multitasking during commute)
- Saves favorites to share on Instagram stories (with attribution)

**Guided Journeys (Weekly):**
- Uses guided journeys 1-2x/week, especially themes of "grief," "healing," "surrender"
- Appreciates 5-minute format (fits before bed or during lunch break)

**Masnavi Library (Monthly Exploration):**
- Browses when emotionally activated (after hard workday, during processing)
- Searches by life situation ("loss," "heartbreak," "courage")
- Reads verses that speak to her current experience

**Conversion Trigger:**
- Hits paywall trying to access guided journey (Day 2-4)
- Hesitates at $69.99/year (tight budget)

  1. 14-day free trial lets her test value

  3. Clear positioning as supporting authentic tradition vs. corporate wellness
- May also convert to support BIPOC/POC access to spiritual resources

**Long-Term Retention:**
- Medium-high retention (60-70% renewal) if:
  - Content feels authentic and spiritually nourishing (not clinical/corporate)
  - New journeys added that address real-life struggles (trauma, justice, grief)
  - App remains affordable or offers hardship pricing
- Risk of churn if:
  - Price increases
  - App pivots to corporate wellness partnerships (feels like sellout)
  - Better free alternatives with diverse voices emerge

### Design Implications for Aisha
- **Visual beauty + substance**: Instagram-worthy design but real depth (not performative)
- **Diverse voices**: If audio narration added, must include BIPOC narrators
- **Context and education**: Teach her about Sufi tradition, Rumi's life, Persian culture (respectful learning)
- **Shareable moments**: Easy to screenshot/share daily verse (with attribution) to Instagram
- **Affordability**: Consider sliding scale, nonprofit discount, or scholarship program
- **No corporate wellness**: Avoid language of "productivity" or "optimization"
- **Authenticity signals**: Show who created content, how tradition is honored, where money goes

- **Comparison features**: Side-by-side translations if multiple versions available
- **Context always visible**: Book, chapter, verse numbers; surrounding verses; thematic tags
- **Clean, distraction-free reading**: No gamification or wellness language (turns him off)
- **Teaching resources**: Class licenses, student discounts, assignment features (future consideration)

## SEGMENTATION STRATEGY

### Primary Target Segment: **The Depth Seeker** (Maya)
**Why:** Largest addressable market, highest retention, willing to pay, evangelizes to friends

**Acquisition Strategy:**
- App Store keywords: "authentic Rumi," "Sufi poetry," "spiritual depth"

## RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

### Persona Development Process

**1. Secondary Research (Quantitative)**
- National Endowment for the Arts poetry readership surveys (2017-2023)
- Meditation app user analytics (Calm, Headspace, Insight Timer published data)
- Spiritual wellness market reports (Statista, Business of Apps)
- Demographics: Age, gender, education, income, location

**2. Competitive User Research (Observational)**
- App Store reviews analysis (Calm: 100K+ reviews, Headspace: 50K+ reviews)
- Reddit communities (r/meditation, r/spirituality, r/poetry)
- User complaints and feature requests in public forums

**3. Spiritual Seeker Typology (Qualitative Framework)**
- Bhagavad Gita's 4 types of seekers (Material, Knowledge, Distressed, Enlightened)
- Mapped to modern meditation/wellness app motivations

**4. Synthesis & Validation**
- Created composite personas representing observed patterns
- Triangulated data from multiple sources (poetry, meditation, spirituality)
- Grounded each detail in research (not assumptions)

### Data Confidence Levels

**High Confidence:**
- Demographics (age, gender, education) from NEA surveys
- App usage patterns from published analytics
- Price sensitivity from subscription data

**Medium Confidence:**
- Psychographics (motivations, values) from review analysis + typologies
- Pain points from observational research (forums, reviews)

**Noted as Assumption:**
- Specific names, occupations, relationship details (illustrative composites)
- Direct quotes (representative of sentiment, not verbatim user interviews)

### Limitations

1. **No Primary User Interviews**: Personas based on secondary research, not direct Rumi App user testing (app is pre-launch)
2. **Geographic Bias**: U.S.-focused data (NEA surveys, U.S. app analytics)
3. **Platform Assumption**: Research assumes smartphone ownership and app comfort (excludes digitally disconnected seekers)
4. **Evolution Over Time**: Personas reflect 2024-2026 data; user needs will evolve and require updates

### Next Steps for Validation

**Pre-Launch:**
- Interview 5-10 people matching each persona profile
- Test prototypes with representative users
- Validate pain points and feature priorities

**Post-Launch:**
- Analyze actual user behavior via analytics
- Survey subscribers at 30, 60, 90 days
- Refine personas based on real data (not projections)
