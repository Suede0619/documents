## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY: SANCTUARY PRINCIPLES

These principles guide all design work across fundraising deck and app:

### 1. **Depth Without Overwhelm**
- Information is layered, not front-loaded
- Users discover depth when ready
- Progressive disclosure of complexity
- Never "dump" 25,000 verses on someone

### 2. **Wisdom Without Dogma**
- No prescriptive "you should" language
- Rumi speaks for himself
- App facilitates, doesn't preach
- Respect user's own spiritual path

### 3. **Beauty Without Distraction**
- Design serves content, never competes
- No flashy effects or trendy gradients
- Timeless aesthetic, not trendy
- Silence and space are design elements

### 4. **Guidance Without Gamification**
- No streaks, badges, or points
- No social comparison or leaderboards
- No manipulative notifications
- Gentle nudges, never nagging

### 5. **Authenticity Without Appropriation**
- Respect Islamic and Sufi traditions
- Don't commodify or "brand" Rumi
- Avoid Orientalist tropes
- Let scholarship and poetry stand on their own

### 6. **Technology as Vessel, Not Vehicle**
- AI supports, doesn't replace teaching
- Features enable contemplation, not consumption
- Offline-first where possible
- Privacy-respecting (no surveillance capitalism)

## RISKS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Content Overwhelm
**Problem:** 25,000 verses can paralyze users
**Mitigation:**
- Curated daily verse as default entry point
- Guided journeys provide structure
- Search and themes make library navigable
- Progressive disclosure of depth

### Risk 2: Cultural Appropriation
**Problem:** Western company commodifying Islamic/Sufi tradition
**Mitigation:**
- John's 40+ years in tradition provides authenticity
- Scholarly translations, not New Age interpretations
- Respect for source material in design and copy
- Avoid Orientalist aesthetics (no literal arabesques, "exotic" music, etc.)

### Risk 3: Spiritual Bypass
**Problem:** App becomes "spiritual entertainment" not transformation
**Mitigation:**
- Design for contemplation, not consumption
- No gamification or engagement hacking
- Encourage offline reflection
- "Sanctuary not feed" principle

### Risk 4: Freemium Tension
**Problem:** Paywall disrupts contemplative experience
**Mitigation:**
- Generous free tier (daily verse is core value)
- Premium feels like "going deeper" not "unlocking basics"
- Pricing accessible ($[X]/month is less than a coffee)
- Never manipulative language

### Risk 5: Platform Dependence
**Problem:** Apple/Google policies or fees undermine business
**Mitigation:**
- Build email list (if user consents)
- Consider web app version (future)
- Diversify to Android, not iOS-only
- Plan for platform changes
