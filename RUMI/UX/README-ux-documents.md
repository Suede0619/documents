### **2. User Testing Logic**
**File:** `User Testing Logic-20260112140541.md`
**Timeline:** Ongoing (post-launch)
**Stage:** Post-launch optimization

#### What it is:
Your consultant's A/B testing framework for systematically improving key metrics through data-driven experiments.

#### When to use:
**AFTER** you launch MVP and have 500+ real users generating meaningful data.

#### Workflow:
```
Launch MVP
  â†“
Collect Baseline Data (2-4 weeks)
  â†“
Identify High-Impact Test (prioritize by leverage)
  â†“
Design Variants (A vs. B)
  â†“
Run Test (1-2 weeks for statistical significance)
  â†“
Analyze Results
  â†“
Ship Winner or Iterate
  â†“
Repeat (continuous optimization)
```

#### Test categories covered:
1. **Onboarding Flow Tests**
   - Login options (email vs. social login)
   - Number of onboarding steps
   - Progress indicators and checklists

2. **Paywall and Pricing Tests**
   - Paywall layout and copy
   - Price-point testing
   - Trial length and offer framing
   - Plan structure (monthly vs. annual)

3. **Navigation and In-App UX Tests**
   - Primary navigation structure
   - CTA position and prominence
   - Empty states and helper UI

4. **Messaging, Nudges, and Push Tests**
   - Push notification copy
   - Send time and trigger rules
   - In-app upsell prompts

#### Key metrics tracked:
- **Activation:** Onboarding completion, time-to-first-value
- **Engagement:** D1/D7/D30 retention, session frequency
- **Monetization:** Paywall conversion, ARPU, LTV, churn
- **Quality:** App Store rating, crashes, support tickets

#### What you get:
Systematic, data-driven improvements to conversion, retention, and revenue through proven testing patterns.

#### Budget required:
Analytics tools (Mixpanel/Amplitude: $0-200/month free tier), Todd's dev time for implementing variants

### Q: Can we skip the Lean MVP Plan and go straight to Comprehensive Plan?
**A:** Only if you have:
- 3-4 months before you need to launch
- $20K-50K budget to hire help
- Luxury of doing extensive user research

If you're pre-seed and need to move fast, the Lean MVP Plan is designed for you.

---

### Q: Can we do A/B testing before launch?
**A:** No. You need real users (500+) for meaningful A/B tests. Pre-launch, focus on qualitative feedback (guerrilla testing with 5 users is in Week 4 of Lean MVP Plan).

---

### Q: What if we launch MVP but don't get to 500 users?
**A:** You can still run tests with 100-200 users, but:
- Results won't be as statistically significant
- Takes longer to reach significance (3-4 weeks vs. 1-2 weeks)
- Prioritize highest-impact tests (onboarding, paywall)

---

### Q: When do we switch from Lean MVP Plan to Comprehensive Plan?
**A:** When you have:
- âœ… Raised seed funding ($250K+)
- âœ… 1,000+ active users (validated product-market fit)
- âœ… Time/budget to invest in scaling (not rushing to next funding milestone)

If you hit these criteria at Month 6, switch to Comprehensive Plan for Android, advanced features, etc.

---

### Q: Do we need all three documents?
**A:** Keep all three as reference, but:
- **Now:** Focus 100% on Lean MVP Plan
- **Post-launch:** Switch focus to User Testing Logic
- **Post-seed:** Switch focus to Comprehensive Plan

Think of them as a three-stage roadmap for your UX journey.

---

### Q: What if our timeline slips?
**A:** The Lean MVP Plan has contingencies:
- **If Week 1-2 takes longer:** Reduce screens (6-7 core screens instead of 8-10)
- **If Week 3 takes longer:** Use iOS design templates instead of designing from scratch
- **If Week 4 takes longer:** Skip guerrilla testing, go straight to handoff

Quality bar: "Good enough to validate" not "pixel perfect"

---
