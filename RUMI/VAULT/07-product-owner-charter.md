# Product Owner Charter

Rumi App - CPO/Product Owner Role Definition  
Last Updated: January 21, 2026

---

## Overview

Defines the Product Owner role, authority, and decision-making scope. Critical for controlling MVP iterations based on customer feedback.

---

## Role Definition

**Title:** Product Owner (CPO Function)  
**Current Assignment:** Yalor Mewn (COO serving as interim Product Owner)  
**Reports To:** John Wyrick (CEO)  
**Collaborates With:** Todd Bowden (CTO), Stuart Paul (Design)  

---

## Purpose

The Product Owner is the single point of accountability for:
- What gets built
- In what order
- Based on customer feedback and business priorities

This role prevents design-by-committee and ensures rapid, focused iteration.

---

## Decision-Making Authority

### Full Authority (No Approval Needed)

| Decision Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Feature prioritization | "Journey X ships before Journey Y" |
| Sprint scope | "We cut feature Z this sprint" |
| Bug priority | "This is P1, fix immediately" |
| UX trade-offs | "Simpler flow beats more options" |
| Beta group selection | "Add these 20 users to Group A" |
| Feedback triage | "This request goes to backlog" |

### Requires CEO Consultation

| Decision Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Major feature cuts | Removing announced features |
| Pricing changes | Any subscription price adjustments |
| Content tone | Anything affecting spiritual integrity |
| Platform priorities | iOS vs Android timing |
| Public commitments | Press, investor communications |

### Requires Team Consensus

| Decision Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Roadmap changes | Q3 vs Q4 feature moves |
| New content verticals | Adding Hafez, Shams timing |
| Major UX overhauls | Complete redesign of core flows |
| Hiring priorities | Which role to fill first |

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Backlog Management

- Maintain prioritized product backlog
- Write clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Ensure backlog is visible and understood by team
- Say "no" to requests that dilute core mission

### 2. Customer Feedback Loop

- Review all beta feedback weekly
- Talk to users directly (minimum [X] per month)
- Translate feedback into actionable items
- Track feature requests and patterns

### 3. Sprint Planning

- Define sprint goals and scope
- Make trade-off calls when capacity is limited
- Ensure Definition of Done is clear
- Accept or reject completed work

### 4. Stakeholder Communication

- Communicate priorities to team
- Report progress to CEO/stakeholders
- Manage expectations on timelines
- Align marketing with product capabilities

### 5. Metrics Ownership

- Define success metrics for features
- Track and report on product KPIs
- Use data to inform prioritization
- Run A/B tests and analyze results

## Relationship to Other Roles

### With CEO (John Wyrick)
- Product Owner proposes priorities
- CEO has veto on spiritual integrity issues
- Weekly 1:1 to align on direction
- CEO sets vision, PO executes

### With CTO (Todd Bowden)
- PO defines "what" and "why"
- CTO defines "how" and "when"
- Joint ownership of sprint commitments
- CTO advises on technical feasibility

### With Design Lead (Stuart Paul)
- Collaborative on UX decisions
- PO has final call on UX trade-offs
- Design proposes, PO disposes
- Joint ownership of user experience

### With Advisor (Ben Rewis)
- PO presents product progress monthly
- Advisor provides strategic input
- PO incorporates advice as appropriate

## Escalation Process

### Level 1: Product Owner Decision
- Most day-to-day decisions
- Response time: Immediate

### Level 2: CEO Consultation
- Significant scope or direction changes
- Response time: 24-48 hours

### Level 3: Team Discussion
- Major strategic shifts
- Response time: Next team meeting

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Product Owner Should NOT:
- ❌ Make technical architecture decisions (CTO domain)
- ❌ Override spiritual/content tone (CEO domain)
- ❌ Ignore customer feedback for personal preferences
- ❌ Commit to dates without CTO input
- ❌ Design UI/UX (Design Lead domain)
- ❌ Say yes to everything

### Product Owner SHOULD:
- ✅ Say "no" frequently and clearly
- ✅ Prioritize ruthlessly
- ✅ Be customer-obsessed
- ✅ Make fast decisions with incomplete data
- ✅ Change mind when evidence warrants
- ✅ Protect the team from scope creep
