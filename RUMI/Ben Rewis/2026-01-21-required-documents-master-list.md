# Rumi App - Required Documents Master List

Pre-Seed Startup Documentation Requirements  
Per Ben Rewis Strategy Session, January 21, 2026

---

## Overview

This document outlines all critical documents needed for the Rumi App as a pre-seed startup. These documents serve three purposes: operational clarity, investor readiness, and team alignment. Each document is listed with its executive summary explaining what it does and why it matters.

---

## 1. Cash Flow Forecast

**Status:** Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical  
**Owner:** TBD

### Executive Summary

A month-by-month projection of money coming in (revenue) and money going out (expenses) over 12-24 months. This is the single most important financial document for a pre-seed startup.

### Purpose

- Shows how long current cash will last (runway)
- Identifies the fiscal gap requiring investor funding
- Enables informed decisions about spending and hiring
- Required for any serious investor conversation
- Helps all team members understand monthly burn rate

### Key Components

- Monthly revenue projections (weighted by probability)
- Fixed costs (salaries, subscriptions, infrastructure)
- Variable costs (marketing, contractors)
- Cash on hand tracking
- Runway calculation (months until zero)

---

## 2. Weighted Sales Forecast

**Status:** Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical  
**Owner:** TBD

### Executive Summary

A revenue projection that multiplies expected revenue by the probability of it actually occurring. More realistic than simple revenue projections.

### Purpose

- Provides honest revenue expectations
- Accounts for uncertainty in early-stage sales
- Shows investors you understand risk
- Drives hiring and spending decisions

### Key Components

- Revenue scenarios (conservative, moderate, aggressive)
- Probability weights for each scenario
- Weighted average calculation
- Monthly and quarterly rollups

---

## 3. RACI Matrix

**Status:** Not Started  
**Priority:** High  
**Owner:** TBD

### Executive Summary

A responsibility assignment chart defining who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for every key task and decision. Ensures "one throat to choke" accountability.

### Purpose

- Clarifies decision-making authority
- Eliminates confusion about who does what
- Prevents tasks from falling through cracks
- Drives organizational structure and hiring
- Required for scaling beyond founding team

### Key Components

- List of all major tasks/decisions
- Team member assignments (R/A/C/I)
- One and only one "A" per task
- Regular review and updates

---

**Status:** Not Started  
**Priority:** High  
**Owner:** TBD

### Executive Summary

Visual diagram showing current team structure and projected hires post-funding. Shows reporting relationships and how the team will scale.

### Purpose

- Shows investors you have a scaling plan
- Clarifies reporting structure
- Identifies gaps in current team
- Drives hiring priorities

### Key Components

- Projected hires (with funding)
- Timeline for new hires
- Reporting relationships

## 5. Job Descriptions

**Status:** Not Started  
**Priority:** Medium  
**Owner:** TBD

### Executive Summary

Detailed descriptions of each role needed, both current and future. Defines responsibilities, requirements, and success metrics.

### Purpose

- Success metrics

## 6. Release Train Schedule

**Status:** Not Started  
**Priority:** High  
**Owner:** TBD (Product Owner/CPO)

### Executive Summary

A weekly cadence document defining when releases happen, when testing occurs, and when feedback is integrated. Creates predictable product development rhythm.

### Purpose

- Creates accountability for shipping
- Ensures regular customer feedback integration
- Prevents scope creep
- Aligns team on expectations

### Key Components

- Thursday: Weekly release (MVP n+1)
- Friday: Beta testing (A/B/C groups)
- Monday: Feedback integration
- Tuesday: Product release discussion
- Wednesday: Team QA
