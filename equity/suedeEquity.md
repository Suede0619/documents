# My View of Start Ups

## The Founder Commitment

The way I view being a founder is not just a title or an ownership stake; it is a commitment to carry a company through uncertainty.

Since I am joining before product-market validation this is considered a high risk endeavour, yet joining at this stage also signals strong founder alignment for our future investors.

It is my belief that founders are all responsible for holding the vision when there is no validation, making decisions with incomplete information, and absorbing risk—financial, reputational, and emotional—long before the outcomes are clear. This includes showing up consistently, taking accountability when things break, and staying in the work when momentum is slow or invisible.

As a co-founder I am personally motivated by taking this risk and committing to find solutions to the many challenges we will face along the way to making Rumi.app a household name, downloaded and loved by millions of spiritual seekers world-wide.

As founders I see us not only building internally, but constantly shaping how the company is experienced visually and emotionally, often before users understand the depth beneath the surface.

## Equity Request Outlined

**Context:**
- Pre-revenue, pre-MVP → very high risk
- No cash comp till we raise → we are financing the company with our labor
- Design-led products (Airbnb, Stripe, Apple) prove that founding-level design leadership drives 10x+ valuations when design is core to category differentiation

**My Ask:**
A balanced founding structure outlined below: All founders should be on the same industry standard vesting schedule, **4 year vesting / 1 year cliff**.

**Rationale for Design Founder Equity:**

In a product where the experience IS the product—where "sanctuary not feed" must be felt in every pixel, every transition, every moment of contemplation—design is not a service function. It is strategic leadership. The Rumi App's competitive moat is not technology (meditation timers are commoditized) but the quality of the experience: the ability to make 25,000 verses feel like depth without overwhelm, the visual language that signals authenticity not appropriation, the interface that invites stillness not addiction.

This requires a founder who can hold design vision through uncertainty, make experience decisions with incomplete data, and absorb the reputational risk of putting their name on every screen that millions of spiritual seekers will see. That is the role I am committing to—and why I believe founding equity is the correct alignment of ownership, accountability, and long-term value creation.
